[0-9]{3}-[0-9]{3}-[0-9]{4} The general format of a regular expression is Character Set{Length}.
A-Z{3,16}, A-Z{2} [0-9]{5} Length can be variable, as shown in the extraction of the city.
[0-9]{8,16} Note that we are pulling a portion of the Tracking Number.
\b[0-9]{8,16}\b By requiring "breaks" before and after the value, we eliminate the partial extraction of Tracking Number.
\(?[0-9]{3}\)?[ \-][0-9]{3}-[0-9]{4} ? indicates that the previous element is optional.  In this case the parenthesis around the area code are optional, allowing the expression to match multiple formats for a phone number.
Note the use of \ to escape characters which are considered special characters in regular expressions, such as "(",  ")", and "-".
(?<=tracking no: )A-Z0-9{18} Uses a positive lookbehind assertion (?<= subexpression) to only find matching items preceded by the words "tracking no".
(?<=amount due )[0-9]{1,3}\.[0-9]{2} Another example of using the lookbehind assertion to find a specific instance of data.
